Cleric Class

Characteristics

  • Weapon Type: Wand, Flail, Mace (reach, swing speed, attack combo)
  • Secondary: Shield
  • Strong AoE % health heal
  • Deals light damage
  • Very slow but generally tankier than all other classes
  • Can semi-stun enemies through electrocution
  • Can boost healing of all sources

(Aaron)         As a saint main, I really gotta say the Cleric base class is quite lacking, but luckily you only have to deal with it until level 15. The damage skills given aren't especially impressive but with it's solid healing and tankiness clerics tend to have the best survivability...when it doesn't need to move fast, as their tumble is horrible. However, the Cleric's specializations improve them to become either the magic damaging Priest or the mixed damage Paladin, both of which can do and buff light damage.

The Priest divides into the electricity blasting Inquisitor, which is a strong light damage dps class, or the relic support Saint, which has a wide variety of extra buffs, utilities, and heals. Priests also have three strong buffs that increase both damage and damage reduction.

Paladins on the other hand can stun, reduce damage to themselves, taunt enemies, and also electrocute. Crusaders specifically take mainly damage abilities and can dish out high light damage DPS, while Guardians become massive tanks that can protect allies with stuns and shields. They also benefit from both physical and magic damage alike.

(Kody)

        

Notable Skills:

Righteous Bolt-an electric ground slam that sends small bolts outward, electrocuting enemies. Out of the four main damaging abilties clerics have, this is probably the only one that really does damage, and it's typically leveled up by nearly all the cleric specializations/players.

However, after playing Saint for a long time I've decided in most cases it's not especially worth taking on priests. Saints already don't do that much dps to begin with and having this extremely close-range ability isn't worth the time or effort to use. Inquisitors, who specialize in dps may have more use for it but I still don't find it particularly valuable on them either. At least Lightning Bolt, the first priest skill, is ranged and can reduce light defense making it much more practical even though it doesn't hit as hard. Paladins on the other hand can make much more use of this ability since they're always up close to the enemy, and can most definitely max it, but if you're a priest, only take it if you feel like you're using it often. Otherwise, I'd drop it for Toughness and more mana regen (after lvl 15! pre 15 take any skills you like to get you there. After that, your new skills are way better)

Aura of Healing-an AoE percent health heal, making it most useful in party situations. Beware of its 60s cooldown, however, that makes it unreliable but still good in dangerous situations. Not crazy strong when soloing but use it whenever you need to.

Block-a buff that will block a flat number of incoming attacks (can still move freely). After casting it, any attack that isn't a status ailment will be blocked by your shield, but since it happens automatically, it could potentially cancel whatever you're doing if you time things incorrectly. Guardians will typically take this instead of Righteous Bolt. Also, it only lasts 20 seconds.

Toughness-a passive that makes you take less physical damage (10% maxed). A lot of cleric builds will not take this in favor of more points on Righteous Bolt, but like I said before this is probably more worth it if you aren't using RB very often or don't do much damage either way.

Sliding Step, Vengeance Strike, and Aerial Evasion- utility moves; roll to dodge attacks, pin wheel to knock back opponents when getting up, aerial evasion to recover in the air (decent cooldown).

It's worth mentioning that Sliding Step is a joke; this cleric version of Tumble barely covers any distance and isn't useful for actually getting around the map, but just for side stepping boss attacks (and just barely!). If you really want to get around the map, hit Tab to put your weapon away, and just be free.
