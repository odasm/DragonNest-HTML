Tinkerer Class

Characteristics

  • Weapon Type: Bubble Blaster, Cannon
  • Secondary: Powerglove
  • Has a diversity of damage types through missiles, turrets, bombs, and fire/ice/poison abilities
  • Can deal nearly all damage elements depending on specialization
  • Average to above-average mobility
  • Features summons that deal and tank damage, with a little cc
  • Squishy, and has all types of attack ranges

(Aaron)         In terms of specializations, Tinkerers seem to branch off in all different directions in terms of ranged characters. The base class itself features the robot butler Alfredo and several grenades, and branches off into using turrets, bombs, missiles, and cannons, or magma waves, ice beams, poison bombs, and bubble blasters (sometimes syringes). In my opinion, Tinkerers are the most well-rounded and has no low-tier specializations; all 4 options are solid in their respective areas.

Engineers have an arsenal of turrets and quackums (robot ducks) that go along with Alfredo for nice dps. Their robot army and missiles provide some cc coverage in most cases, as they can slow or knock up small enemies. They have 1 defensive party buff that redirects damage to Alfredo. Engineers typically set up their army and proceed to decimate enemies with their explosive weapons. They are entirely physical damage.

Alchemists are completely different from Engineers in that all their abilities are elemental concoctions with zero machines or cannon blasts. On one side they have explosive fire abilities (burn DoT) and ice blasts (freeze or slow), and on the other they have poison bombs (poison DoT) and healing abilities. They have 1 solid party damage buff, and a few resist debuffs (until you 2nd specialize). They also have noticeable mobility for a ranged class with their double dash. After charging bubbles, Alchemists and zip right into their enemies and throw out fast burst combos. They are entirely magic damage.*

(Kody)

        

Notable Skills:

Napalm and Star Burst- both throw grenades with different effects. Napalm explodes with fire and burns enemies, while Star Burst has a chance to stun them. Before 1st specialization, these are pretty nice aoe abilities for killing mobs, but they lose their usefulness after specializing. Both are physical damage and Engineers don't need them (unless PVP).

Wax- drops a puddle of wax on the ground that trips enemies and speeds up allies. It's a nice utility ability that can keep enemies at bay when the mob is bigger than you can handle. Pretty much every Tinkerer will take/max this ability, but whether or not you need or want to use it is up to you.

Summon Alfredo and Recall Alfredo-drops the robot butler straight out of the sky, and brings him back to your position from no matter where he is. The drop itself doesn't do anything except make Alfredo appear, but he's great for drawing aggro and dealing some damage.

Since he moves fairly slowly and often ends up in strange areas while chasing enemies, Recall Alfredo brings him back to where you're standing. This ability can also be used strategically, such as when Alfredo activiates one of his Engineer abilities. Speaking of which, he only gets additional abilities as an Engineer, making him only mildly useful for Alchemists, but still nice for Tinkerers to knock enemies around and take damage.

Yum-a-tron- summons a... like fire hydrant-looking machine that pops out random food. The food is either buffs or hp/mp restorers, but usually only about 10% at a time. It's nice to have in the middle of a hard dungeon and you're low on life or mana, but usually isn't needed that much. What's interesting is that it doesn't cost an actual skill point; it just has a level requirement.

Roto whirl, Rise and Shine, and Aerial Evasion- utility moves; fly away to dodge attacks, rising blast to knock back opponents when getting up, aerial evasion to recover in the air (decent cooldown). Alchemists have the ability to Roto whirl twice in a row at times.
